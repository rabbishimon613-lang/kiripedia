**Edward "Skip" Gnehm** was the U.S. Ambassador to Kuwait at the time of the February 1991 liberation. Per [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou): *"One of the finest men I've ever worked for anywhere in the world. … He loved Kuwait as much as he loved the United States."*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="35:08" /><Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:47:37" />

## Liberation Day

Gnehm led the reopening of the U.S. embassy in Kuwait City. Per Kiriakou, the first cable he sent read: *"American Embassy Kuwait City is now open for business."* Kiriakou: *"Just reading that sentence made me think, 'We've won.'"*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:29:18" /><Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:29:50" />

## The pro-American graffiti problem

Per Kiriakou, Gnehm said once — a line that *"stuck in my mind all these 35 years"* — *"I have a problem that's unique among American ambassadors anywhere in the world. I can't get people to stop writing pro-American graffiti on the side of the embassy."*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="35:08" /><Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="35:40" />
