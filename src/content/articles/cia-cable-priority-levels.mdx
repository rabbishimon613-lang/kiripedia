**CIA cable priority levels** are the five tiers of immediacy assigned to U.S. government cable traffic. Per [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou) on the Bidoun Waraq Podcast, ascending from lowest to highest:<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:18:53" /><Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:19:23" />

- **Routine** — *"Read it or don't read it. Nobody cares. Maybe it says, you know, Faisal is coming on a visit in three weeks. Okay, thank you. Nobody cares."*
- **Priority** — *"Okay, there's some diplomatic business here, you should read this sometime today."*
- **Immediate** — *"This is important. Read it now."*
- **Flash** — *"Emergency."*
- **CRITIC** — *"They're coming over the embassy walls, God save."*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:18:53" /><Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:19:23" />

## Frequency in normal life vs. wartime

Per Kiriakou: *"Most people could go through a 30-year career and never see a CRITIC. We were getting multiple CRITICs every day."* During the 1991 Gulf War the NSA was transmitting CRITICs each time it detected an Iraqi missile launch — *"NSA would say 'CRITIC: hostile missile launch,' and then it would have the geo coordinates."* Kiriakou kept a wall-sized map of Iraq over his desk; on each CRITIC he would call out the coordinates, the office would gather around, and they would project — by launch site — whether the target was Israel or Saudi Arabia/Bahrain.<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:19:23" /><Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="1:19:55" />
