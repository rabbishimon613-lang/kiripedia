**Judge William Webster** was the Director of Central Intelligence during the 1990 invasion of Kuwait. He had previously served as a federal judge and as Director of the FBI — the only person to have served as both FBI and CIA director. Per [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou): *"He was just a very kind, nice man."*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="50:13" />

During the Kuwait task force Kiriakou — a junior analyst working 12-hour shifts on a six-week rotation — became a regular briefer to Webster. His introduction was abrupt. The shift leader: *"Have you ever briefed the director?"* Kiriakou: *"I've never even seen the director."* *"Well, you're going to brief the director in 15 minutes, so be prepared."* It was 4 a.m. Webster: *"What's happening in Kuwait?"*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="50:13" /><Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="50:44" />
