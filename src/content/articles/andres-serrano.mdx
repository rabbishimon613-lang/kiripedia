**Andres Serrano** is the American artist best known for Piss Christ, the 1980s photograph of a beaker of urine containing an inverted crucifix. Per [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou), the work *"made Ronald Reagan so angry that he cut off funding to the [National Endowment for the Arts](/wiki/national-endowment-for-democracy)."* Kiriakou's gloss: *"All it did was it made Andres even more famous."*<Cite s="2026-05-25-covert-operations-insight-chills" t="01:40" /><Cite s="2026-05-25-covert-operations-insight-chills" t="02:11" />

## The Tate Modern torture series

Serrano subsequently photographed Kiriakou as one of a series of subjects in a project on torture chambers and torture around the world. Kiriakou's portrait is held in the permanent collection of Tate Modern, London. Per Kiriakou: *"It's in a series of pictures from torture chambers around the world, and it's in the Tate Modern. In London. Isn't that hilarious? And it's next to like this — a torturer from the US military."*<Cite s="2026-05-25-covert-operations-insight-chills" t="02:11" /><Cite s="2026-05-25-covert-operations-insight-chills" t="02:44" />
