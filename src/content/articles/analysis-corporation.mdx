**The Analysis Corporation** was a private intelligence firm in Arlington, Virginia, created — along with its companion entity [Arrais Corporation](/wiki/arrais-corporation) — as an LLC pass-through for retired [Senior Intelligence Service](/wiki/cia) (SIS) personnel from the [Central Intelligence Agency](/wiki/cia)'s analytical side. *"The analysis company was where all the SIS senior intelligence service level analysts would go, the retired analysts."* [John Brennan](/wiki/john-brennan) was appointed its CEO in 2005.<Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="50:10" /><Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="50:40" />

## Origin and post-9/11 commercialization

The two firms were originally configured as informal social-and-tax pass-throughs: *"They were legitimately set up just as LLC pass-throughs, right? So people could go and smoke cigars and drink coffee and tell stories with their friends and hang out all day."* After September 11, both firms became substantially commercial. [Arrais Corporation](/wiki/arrais-corporation) — the parallel pass-through for retired operations officers — was subsequently acquired by IBM for over $1 billion, making senior CIA retirees *"not just rich but … like, you know, megamillions lotto kind of rich."*<Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="50:40" /><Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="51:11" />
