**Saud Nasir Al-Sabah** was Kuwait's Ambassador to the United States in the run-up to the 1990 Iraqi invasion. Per [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou): *"Ambassador Saud Nasir was just a lovely, lovely man, very generous with his time. We had a very good close relationship with him."*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="5:15" />

Kiriakou, then an overt CIA analyst, was a regular visitor at the Kuwaiti embassy: *"I would go and say, 'Hello, Mr. Ambassador. I'm John Kiriakou from the CIA.' He knew me as that kid from the CIA."*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="5:46" />

In June and July 1990 the CIA's warnings to Kuwait were communicated *"two ways. We would do it through the Kuwaiti embassy, which would have been Ambassador Saud Nasir Al-Sabah, and then in Kuwait the ambassador would have gone to the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Minister of Defense."*<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="13:06" />

On the morning of the August 2, 1990 invasion President George H. W. Bush was on the phone with the ambassador when Kiriakou and his boss arrived in the Oval Office.<Cite s="2026-02-26-bidoun-waraq-kuwait-invasion" t="28:27" />
