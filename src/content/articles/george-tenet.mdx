**George J. Tenet** served as Director of Central Intelligence from 1997 to 2004, the longest serving DCI since [William J. Casey](/wiki/bill-casey). His tenure spanned the September 11 attacks and the launch of the [enhanced interrogation techniques](/wiki/enhanced-interrogation) program; in [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou)'s account he is the figure most directly responsible for the agency's early-2000s institutional posture, and the patron — the *"rabbi"* in agency parlance — of [John Brennan](/wiki/john-brennan)'s subsequent rise.

## The Brennan rabbi relationship

Per Kiriakou — who has known Brennan personally since 1990 — Brennan's institutional advancement inside the CIA was structurally dependent on Tenet's patronage. *"He had a rabbi in George Tenet. And the next thing you know, he's at the top of the heap."* The relationship is the institutional explanation Kiriakou offers for Brennan's rise despite what Kiriakou characterizes as a clear personality profile — *"the archetypal sociopath"* — that the agency's selection process should otherwise have flagged.<Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="47:36" /><Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="48:07" />

## EIT acceptance

In late October 2001 — at a cocktail party in the days following the September 11 attacks — Tenet personally accepted the pitch from contract psychologists [Mitchell and Jessen](/wiki/mitchell-and-jessen) for what became the [enhanced interrogation techniques](/wiki/enhanced-interrogation) program. The agency signed the Mitchell and Jessen contract in January 2002. The first detainee on whom the program was applied was [Abu Zubaydah](/wiki/abu-zubaydah), captured by [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou)'s team in Pakistan in March 2002.<Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="55:34" /><Cite s="2026-01-16-julian-dorey-french-intelligence" t="56:05" />

## The Five Eyes file-opening

In the early-2000s reorganization of relationships among the Anglosphere intelligence services after September 11, Tenet personally ordered that the [Five Eyes](/wiki/five-eyes) — the U.S., U.K., Canadian, Australian, and New Zealand services — *"literally open the files"* to each other. The directive established the contemporary practice of Five Eyes liaison officers sitting at each other's headquarters.<Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="3:12:35" /><Cite s="2025-08-31-dalton-fischer-mossad-blackwater" t="3:13:05" />

## The "prism of smoke" quote

Tenet's memoir contains the paraphrased line cited repeatedly by Kiriakou in his characterization of the post-9/11 U.S. national-security mood: *"All major foreign policy decisions after 9/11 were viewed through the prism of smoke from the World Trade Center and the Pentagon."* Kiriakou treats the line as accurate description — *"absolutely true"* — and applies it to his own framing of the moral conduct of senior CIA personnel of the period, including [Jose Rodriguez](/wiki/jose-rodriguez), [James Mitchell](/wiki/mitchell-and-jessen), and [Bruce Jessen](/wiki/mitchell-and-jessen).<Cite s="2023-11-26-dalton-fischer-enhanced-interrogation" t="10:30" />

## Kiriakou's broader assessment

In the Kiriakou framing, Tenet belongs to the senior-CIA-figures-of-the-post-9/11-period cohort — alongside [John McLaughlin](#), [Michael Morell](#), [Jose Rodriguez](/wiki/jose-rodriguez), and Rick Pavitt — whom Kiriakou holds responsible for *"a lot of crimes that were committed in the name of national security."* Kiriakou treats Tenet as categorically distinct from [John Brennan](/wiki/john-brennan), however: Brennan, in Kiriakou's framework, *"plotted against an elected president of the United States,"* which is a different and more dangerous category of conduct than the EIT-era senior leadership's failures.<Cite s="2026-01-16-julian-dorey-french-intelligence" t="33:50" />
