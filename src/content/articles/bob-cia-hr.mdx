<Hatnote>The name *Bob* used in this article is a pseudonym used by John Kiriakou; it is not known to be the man's real name. *"I still to this day have no idea if his name really was Bob."*<Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="17:57" /></Hatnote>

**"Bob"** is a pseudonym referring to the [Central Intelligence Agency](/wiki/cia) officer who served as director of human resources for the entire agency in the late 1980s and personally oversaw [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou)'s recruitment and hiring.<Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="28:27" />

Bob worked out of an unmarked sixth-floor office in [Rosslyn, Virginia](/wiki/rosslyn-virginia), accessed through a hallway door with no name, an inner door secured by a large spin-dial bank-style lock, and a receptionist behind the inner door.<Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="16:53" /> He is described as *"6'6", 320 pounds, loud, smiling — a 'good old guy.'"*<Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="17:57" />

Bob's standard opening question to a candidate was *"Have you ever betrayed a friendship?"*<Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="18:29" /> He routinely reported back to candidates after each stage of the agency's vetting process — psychiatric interview, polygraph — with cheerful confirmation phrased identically across applicants: *"You blew the doors off that meeting."* / *"You aced it."* / *"They're fighting over you at headquarters."*<Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="21:38" /><Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="22:10" /><Cite s="2023-11-12-dalton-fischer-kiriakou-part-1" t="28:27" />
