**John O'Neill** was the FBI's Special Agent in Charge of the New York field office for counterterrorism in the run-up to September 11, 2001. Per [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou): *"John O'Neill was a very brash, flawed guy at FBI counterterrorism New York. … All his flaws aside, [he was] spot-on about Osama bin Laden — 100 percent correct."*<Cite s="2024-11-13-julian-dorey-250" t="1:04:34" /><Cite s="2024-11-13-julian-dorey-250" t="1:05:07" />

Kiriakou identifies O'Neill as the mentor of [Ali Soufan](/wiki/ali-soufan), the FBI Arabic translator who would later become the principal FBI interrogator of [Abu Zubaydah](/wiki/abu-zubaydah). *"He came up under John O'Neill, exactly. And listen, if you can work under John O'Neill and thrive, then you're somebody special."*<Cite s="2024-11-13-julian-dorey-250" t="2:04:34" /><Cite s="2024-11-13-julian-dorey-250" t="2:05:04" />

O'Neill is the principal subject of Lawrence Wright's *The Looming Tower*, which Kiriakou calls *"a classic book, excellent book."*<Cite s="2024-11-13-julian-dorey-250" t="1:04:34" />
