**Ramzi bin al-Shibh** is one of the alleged co-conspirators in the September 11 attacks. He has been declared clinically insane by the U.S. Defense Department's psychiatrist as a direct result of the [Central Intelligence Agency](/wiki/cia)'s use of [sleep deprivation](/wiki/sleep-deprivation) against him. The Defense Department has, as of late August 2023, stated that he **cannot be tried**.<Cite s="2023-11-26-dalton-fischer-enhanced-interrogation" t="05:43" />

## Cause of insanity

> Just recently — we're filming this in early September, the last week of August of 2023 — the Defense Department admitted that they cannot try Ramzi bin al-Shibh, one of the 9/11 co-conspirators. They cannot try him because he's insane. He's clinically insane. Why is he insane? Because of sleep deprivation … This is the Pentagon's psychiatrist who's saying he's insane, not his defense attorneys.<Cite s="2023-11-26-dalton-fischer-enhanced-interrogation" t="05:43" /><Cite s="2023-11-26-dalton-fischer-enhanced-interrogation" t="06:16" />

The diagnosis comes from the U.S. government's own psychiatric staff, not from bin al-Shibh's defense. The CIA was authorized to keep prisoners awake for up to **12 days** continuously — a duration well beyond the seven-day threshold at which the American Psychological Association literature establishes the onset of clinical insanity.<Cite s="2023-11-26-dalton-fischer-enhanced-interrogation" t="03:38" /><Cite s="2023-11-26-dalton-fischer-enhanced-interrogation" t="04:09" />

## Inadmissibility of statements

Independent of his current mental status, no statement bin al-Shibh ever gave can be used against him at trial, because the [CIA](/wiki/cia) tortured the statements out of him.<Cite s="2023-11-26-dalton-fischer-enhanced-interrogation" t="08:54" />
