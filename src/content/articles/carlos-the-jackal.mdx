**Carlos the Jackal** is the Venezuelan-born militant active in the 1970s and 1980s. Per [John Kiriakou](/wiki/john-kiriakou), Carlos was *"the Osama bin Laden of the 70s and 80s"* — a benchmark Kiriakou uses when teaching the history of terrorism, with the recurrent observation that his students *"look at me like they've never ever heard this name before."*<Cite s="2026-05-04-cleared-hot-446-cost-of-truth" t="1:59:10" /><Cite s="2026-05-04-cleared-hot-446-cost-of-truth" t="1:59:40" />

## Athens, the middleman, and London

When Kiriakou was assigned to Athens, his portfolio included tracing the link between Carlos and the Greek group [Revolutionary Organization 17 November](/wiki/revolutionary-organization-17-november). *"What I was trying to do was figure out who the middleman was between Carlos the Jackal and the Greek terrorist group revolutionary organization 17 November. So I got a tip about a guy who was the middleman back in the day. And I found him and he's living in a housing project in London."*<Cite s="2026-05-04-cleared-hot-446-cost-of-truth" t="1:59:40" /><Cite s="2026-05-04-cleared-hot-446-cost-of-truth" t="2:00:12" />

MI6 routed the operation to MI5 — Kiriakou's first encounter with the British domestic service. He flew to London every few weeks for six months working the case. It was during that operation that Kiriakou learned MI5 has no arrest powers and is, per its charter, the British service that *"spies on Britons."*<Cite s="2026-05-04-cleared-hot-446-cost-of-truth" t="2:00:12" /><Cite s="2026-05-04-cleared-hot-446-cost-of-truth" t="2:00:42" />
